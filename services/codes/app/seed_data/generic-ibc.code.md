# Generic Residential Building Code (Illustrative)

> **Illustrative document only.** This text paraphrases common provisions of model building codes
> (IBC/IRC-style) for development and testing of the Floor Plan Studio. It is **not** an adopted
> legal code and must not be relied upon for compliance. Always consult the adopted code of the
> applicable jurisdiction and a licensed professional.

Jurisdiction: Generic (IBC-like) 2021
Effective date: 2021-01-01

## Chapter 10 — Means of Egress

### 1006.2 Egress required

Every habitable space and every dwelling unit shall be provided with access to a means of egress.
Each room or space shall connect, through a continuous and unobstructed path, to an exit that
discharges to a public way. No habitable room shall be arranged so that egress is only possible by
passing through another sleeping room.

### 1010.1.1 Door opening clear width

The minimum clear width of a door opening serving as a component of a means of egress shall be not
less than 815 mm (32 inches), measured between the face of the door and the stop with the door open
90 degrees.

### 1011.5.2 Stair treads and risers

Stair riser height shall be not more than 178 mm (7 inches). Stair tread depth (run) shall be not
less than 280 mm (11 inches). All treads and risers in a single flight shall be of uniform size.

### 1020.2 Corridor width

The minimum clear width of a corridor serving an occupant load of any habitable space shall be not
less than 915 mm (36 inches). Required widths shall be unobstructed by projections.

## Chapter 11 — Accessibility

### 1109.2 Accessible clearances

Accessible bathrooms shall provide a clear floor space permitting a turning circle. The minimum
clear interior dimension of an accessible bathroom shall be not less than 1500 mm to accommodate a
wheelchair turning space.

## Chapter 12 — Interior Environment

### 1204.2 Natural light

Habitable rooms shall be provided with natural light by means of exterior glazed openings with an
area not less than 8 percent of the floor area of the room served.

### 1204.4 Natural ventilation

Habitable rooms shall be provided with natural ventilation by means of openable exterior openings
with an openable area not less than 4 percent of the floor area of the room served.

### 1207.3 Room proportions

To remain usable, a habitable room should not be excessively elongated. The ratio of the longer
plan dimension to the shorter plan dimension of a habitable room should not exceed 3 to 1.

### 1208.1 Minimum room areas

Habitable rooms shall have a floor area of not less than 7 square metres (about 70 square feet).
Every dwelling unit shall have at least one habitable room (a bedroom) with a floor area of not less
than 7 m². Kitchens shall have a floor area of not less than 4.6 square metres. No habitable room,
other than a kitchen, shall be less than 2100 mm in any horizontal plan dimension.

### 1208.2 Minimum ceiling height

Habitable spaces shall have a ceiling height of not less than 2300 mm (7 feet 6 inches) above the
finished floor over the required area of the room.

## Chapter 18 (Zoning) — Site Coverage

### Z-3.1 Building coverage

The building footprint shall not cover more than 60 percent of the site area (maximum lot coverage
ratio of 0.6). Floor area ratio and setbacks are established by the applicable zoning district.
